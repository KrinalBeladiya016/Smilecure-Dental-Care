Exodontia

Tooth extraction is the removal of unrestorable teeth due to tooth decay, periodontal disease, or dental trauma from the socket in the alveolar bone . Teeth that have not erupted through the gum completely or have been fractured under the gum line require surgical dental extraction.

How it works?

Local Anaesthesia & tooth preparation

The dentist numbs the area to be treated by injecting a drug. The tooth is separated from the gum attachment using a periosteal elevator.'

Actual Extraction

Then socket is expanded, and the tooth is further separated using a small or large elevator. Forceps are then used to pull the entire tooth out.

Post extraction socket care

The remaining debris is removed from the socket and it is compressed. The socket is rinsed, and bone edges are filed. Gauze is inserted into the extraction site to control the bleeding.

Post Treatment Care

Pain medication or antibiotics may be prescribed. In case of bigger extraction site, suture might be placed to aid the proper closure of soft tissues. Patient is called after 7-10 days for suture removal.
