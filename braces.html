Orthodontic Treatment

Braces is an orthodontic treatment aimed at improving both the aesthetic & the functional capability of the dentition as a whole . It helps in the correction of misaligned teeth and closing the large gaps between the teeth . It is beneficial for treating underlying problems such as temporomandibular joint dysfunction and bruxism (teeth grinding).

How it works?

Initial Orthodontic Consultation, dental examination & oral prophylaxis

Two X-rays are required to check the position of the teeth & to evaluate the relation of the soft tissues to the bony landmarks according to which the most suitable treatment is advised. Some extractions may be needed to ensure the final perfect alignment. Full mouth examination and oral prophylaxis will be performed for the patient after formulating the final treatment plan.

X-Rays And Impressions

The orthodontist collects the patient X-rays and photographs. To customize the braces, an impression is taken digitally or by biting on a soft impression material.

Braces Application

Each bracket needs to be bonded to the tooth. The tooth surface is first etched, to ensure ideal bonding. The braces are placed on the teeth using a composite material which is then cured. Then the archwire is put through the brackets & tightened. The patient is enlightened about the oral hygiene routine and Dos and Donâ€™ts to prevent any damage.

Follow up appointments

The orthodontist sees the patient every 4-8 weeks throughout the length of the treatment (usually 1-2 years depending on the case) to monitor the treatment progress & make any necessary adjustments, change bands and the archwire. In the last appointment the braces are removed, teeth are polished and retainers are provided for a certain duration.'

Debonding

In the final appointment, the orthodontic brackets and archwire are debonded from the tooth surface. The adhesive resin is removed by polishing the tooth surface and restoring the surface to its original form.

Retainers

The results of the orthodontic treatment are maintained with the help of special appliances called retainers. Your dentist may advise the type of retainer that is most suitable for you to prevent relapse of the treatment.
