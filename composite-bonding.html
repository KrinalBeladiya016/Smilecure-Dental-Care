Tooth restoration or Fillings

Composite bonding is a tooth restoration process that involves bonding a tooth-coloured composite resin to your teeth . A composite resin is a tooth-coloured plastic mixture filled with silicon dioxide which acts as a perfect restorative material due to its insoluble nature, tooth-like appearance, easy manipulation and insensitivity to dehydration.

How it works?

Preparing the tooth

Your dentist will isolate your tooth from the saliva to dry it out and then drill out the infected part of the tooth.

Composite Resin application

Acid etching of the enamel (and dentin) is done and bonding agent is applied. Then composite resin is placed in layers and hardened immediately with a special light.

Shaping the tooth

After the tooth is filled, the composite is shaped and polished to resemble your tooth.
